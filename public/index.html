Welcome to Rock Hawk

Please use the sidebar to navigate

Rock Hawk is an outdoor classroom that has hundreds of educational displays along the 25 miles of trails that surround and lead to the ancient effigy. The displays along the trail and wetlands and the indoor and outdoor interpretive centers near the effigy cover a wide variety of flora and fauna as well as 12,000 years of the area’s history, including a history of Putnam County’s unique large effigies, old cemeteries, home sites, quarries and more. Recreational opportunities include: camping, boating, fishing, archery (3-D & static plus limited hunting), beach, hiking & biking, and nature watching.



The wetlands and the effigy can be observed from viewing towers. The historical and archaeological educational experience is certainly enhanced by the rare combination of a large lake, a major river, wetlands and varied forests and fields that attract a wide variety of wildlife, including over 200 species of birds, at least 25 of which are on the Audubon Society’s Priority Species list. Lockerly Arboretum has labeled a large number of trees and plants along the three-mile Blue trail. Research and other contributions are from major universities and organizations across the US. Join us in a unique recreational and educational experience.



Rules and Guidelines

The Rock Hawk effigy and hiking trails are managed and maintained by the Georgia Power Company. Information is provided from the Georgia Power Company website. The rules and regulations for the recreational areas are as follows:

  • Follow all posted rules.
  • Be considerate of other visitors and the environment.
  • No alcoholic beverages or other intoxicants are allowed.
  • Hours: 7am – 9pm, Sunday – Thursday; 7am – 10pm Friday – Saturday.
Campground Rules
  • Please register before setting up.
  • At the time of registration, please pay for the total number of nights that you will be staying.
  • Your overnight parking fee includes use of day-use facilities during regular hours. (Tallulah Gorge charges separate day-use fee.)
  • Campsite occupancy is limited to 8 people per site.
  • Please place tents, truck campers, motor homes, trailers, etc. on prepared gravel camp pads or driveways to avoid damaging grassed areas.
  • Camping between designated sites is prohibited.
  • Please attach your receipt to the clip on the numbered campsite post at your campsite.
  • Length of stay on the same site is limited to 14 days during a 30-day period except with permission from the park manager.
  • Lantern hangers should be used for lanterns. Please do not hang lanterns on trees.
  • Fires should be confined to grills or fire rings and attended at all times. Dead trees or limbs on ground may be collected for firewood.
  • Do not wash dishes or clothes at drinking faucets or in comfort station sinks.
  • Washers and dryers are provided at some parks.
  • Your RV’s holding tank should be used for sewage and grey water. A sanitary disposal station is provided in the park for your use.
  • Please keep your site clean by placing trash in trash cans or trash dumpster where available.
  • Please keep buildings and grounds clean and neat, and report any unsatisfactory conditions to the park attendant.
    • Campground Hours
  • 7:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m. Sunday through Thursday and 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. Friday and Saturday.
  • Tallulah Gorge campground hours are 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. Monday through Thursday and 7:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. Friday and Saturday.
  • Check-out time is 1:00 p.m.
  • Quiet Hours are 10:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m.
  • Park gates are locked when the park closes each night.
  • Campers returning to the park after the gate is locked may park outside the gate until the park opens the next morning but should not block the road.
    • General Information
  • Possession or use of firearms or fireworks is prohibited.
  • Campers under 18 years of age should be accompanied by an adult.
  • All vehicles, including mini-bikes, are subject to safety requirements and regulations of the Georgia Department of Safety.
  • Visitors of registered campers should register with the Park Attendant to obtain a Day Use Pass and pay a parking fee. Visitors remaining overnight will be charged an additional fee.
  • Pets are welcome. Please keep them on a leash, no longer than six feet, at all times, and please clean up after them.
  • No swimming or fishing at boat ramps or from boat docks.
    • Notice
    To ensure the safety and enjoyment of everyone who visits Georgia Power Recreation Areas, Georgia Power reserves the right to prohibit conduct that the company deems to be inappropriate, offensive, or unsafe. For example, Georgia Power prohibits the display of objects on its campgrounds that:

  • may create a safety hazard or nuisance to persons using the campground
  • may be offensive to others
  • may be inappropriate for viewing by minors
  • unnecessarily restrict a camper’s view of the lake
  • interferes with a camper’s enjoyment of the lake and campground
    • Visitors who engage in conduct the Company deems inappropriate, offensive, or unsafe may be asked to leave the property.
